About

A Million Dollar Question

It’s a regular day. I’m on my way to work, make a stop and pick up my coffee at Starbucks. Standing at the checkout, the thought “Hmm, could’ve saved 3 bucks if I just made coffee at home” crossed my mind, just like any other regular day.

The Problem.

Americans owe nearly 1 Trillion credit card debt, and the average household carries $8,398 in credit card debt. According to the Bureau of Labor and Statistics, $3,000 are spent dining out and $1,100 in coffee shops ($2,000 for millennials).

What we do.

This happened a million times and it is incredibly difficult to make a change in a day-to-day routine. We are often not aware of how much and how frequently we are spending and the immediate reward and satisfaction stemming from small savings are minimal. With Impulses, we are deploying a three step process to make the change happen.

  1. Detect, first utilize user’s bank transaction data to identify “poor” spending habits;
  2. Remind, we notify the users on a regular basis to avoid unnecessary spending;
  3. Incentivize, users that save regularly will be rewarded with virtual coins and lottery!

We would then have the opportunity to channel user’s savings to high yield savings or well-managed investment platforms to promote long-term financial well being for our users.

As inspired students and soon-to-be young professionals, our mission is to help all generations reach long-term financial security and be able to live everyday worry free to fullest potential.
